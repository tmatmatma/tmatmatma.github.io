2013–2016 — illustration, type design
Bloomberg Businessweek Covers

A handful of covers I designed over the years for the business publication. This one, for a story describing Yahoo!’s last ditch effort, is my personal favorite.

At the time moment it began liquidating parts of the company after a long and unsuccessful attempt by then-CEO Marissa Mayer to restore the internet company back to its former glory. Mayer didn’t stay on for too long after this.

What I love about this is that the whole image is a riddle!

Loved using Arial and Times New Roman to create the specific L@@K.

It breaks away completely from the conventional formula of Headline ⇢ Image ⇢ Description.

Most believe that others will experience a fatal system error without this formula.
2018 — creative direction, typographic animation design, layout design
The New York Times, Consent on Campus

(Launch site) This project showcases college students’ own accounts, in their own words, of how they navigate sex and consent in the #MeToo era.

A collab of the Times’ Gender editor and the editor of the popular column Modern Love.

The design strips away imagery and instead uses only typography, negative space and subtle animations.

Powerful computer programming by Umi Syam.

The restraint in the design and helps to surface the power of words and writing — its ability to articulate and to put feelings into focus, especially for events that are complicated and hard to make sense of.

Read more about the design of this project at Journalism.uk.

2017 — illustration
The New York Times, illustrations for Amanda Hess column

In 2017 Alicia DeSantis hired me for a number of illustrations for Amanda Hess’s column about internet culture: revisionist history podcasts, hands, fan-fiction, internet astrology, taboo podcasts, sexual harassment on television, the HQ phenomenon.

They came during the year where I was freelancing entirely for non-editorial clients. Working on these were a boon to my days and made me realize how much I loved working on editorial projects.
2016 — creative direction, design, illustration
Matter Studios, Total Power Move

(PDF) This floppy, self-covered, self-published magazine uses the form and pace of a children’s storybook to tell the story of power on this planet: from the way it exists in nature to the way humans use it.

One big chunky spread at a time, we learn about the way power moves through stuff like oppression, racism, gender constructs, inequality, hate/love/fear/the transient body.

Besides the main big-lettered narrative there are some v cool longform essays woven in there also by Rachel Syme, Ashley Ford, Helena Fitzgerald, Oliver Bateman, Porochista Khakpour.

We traverse all of those themes (and more!) in broad strokes thanks to amazing photo research by Emily Keegin.
